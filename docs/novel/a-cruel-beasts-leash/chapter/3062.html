“Well, I’m just saying,” he said. “That was too bold of you for your first day in this dungeon.”

“How is his condition?”

“Well, I don’t know. Rather, Miss Herman would know better. What poison did you use?”

Emma defensively crossed her arms and ran away to a corner of the prison. “D-Dont come c-closer!” she yelled.

“Why are you so afraid?”

Cleve stood in place and laughed with the knights around him. Emma couldn’t understand what was so funny, but the other knights kept laughing their heads off. Emma faltered and kept backing away. She only realized then, that there was no place for her to go. The prison walls blocked her from all sides; the soldiers blocked her only path of entry or escape. She was overcome with fear and her body instinctively stiffened. It was clearly the same situation as before. At some point, the familiar despair swirled from the tips of her toes to her head.

Meanwhile, Cleve raised his finger at Emma. “Chief Collin is being treated by Mr. Monte. You don’t have to worry because he is an excellent doctor. By the way, time is gold, don’t you agree?”

Emma felt dizzy and her chest was painful. “W-What?” she stuttered, shivering.

“We don’t have much time left.”

“What time?” Emma retorted, trying to buy time.

“LET GO! LET GO! ARGHH!” As Emma screamed and struggled, Cleve slapped the pipette from her hands. It clattered to the floor.
