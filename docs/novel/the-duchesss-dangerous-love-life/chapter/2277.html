“The knights are still in the middle of clearing up the road blockage. They said that it would take at least a day.”

“I see.” Adele pouted and shook her head. Facing the window, she saw a pigeon eating in a feeder.

“Hm?”

He changed his stance and turned his head away, “Be patient, for about a day.”

Noah looked at the table with a bitter look. On top of it were fruits, freshly picked and still had those dewy droplets to them. Over the fireplace was a boiling pot.

Adele looked at the pot, then back at him, her eyes flashing with curiosity.

Noticing her subtle movements, he asked, “Are you hungry?”

“Ah?” She shook her head, “No, but I would like to take a bath.”

Remembering she was in a humble hut with limited facilities, she waved her hands to take back her words. There was no place for her to take a bath anyway.

“Can you stand for a while?”

She moved her foot for a bit, “I think so.”

“Lean on the wall.”

“I… didn’t say anything.”

“Thank you for your understanding.”

She hummed in response, “Wash yourself as much as you want.”

Noah grinned at Adele’s silly words. On the other hand, she concentrated on her bath, thinking that if this atmosphere is to be continued, she might do something embarrassing. As the water got colder, Adele shivered.

Having finished bathing, Noah approached her and stretched out his arms to carry her.

“I can walk…”

Despite Adele’s insistence, he carried her without delay and placed her inside the bathtub.
